What Is Mining

In simple terms, crypto mining is the process of creating the crypto of your choosing (as long as the crypto is POW witch stands for proof of work)by solving cryptographic equations.

One of the most important parts when it comes to mining is the currency you choose to mine. The coin you choose is what your earnings depend on for example as of October 10th 2021 1000mh on Etherium will make you around $2463 a month, Etherium Classic would make one around $1189, and Ravencoin would make one around $3101.

But it is not as simple as that, The amount of GPUs needed differs for each coin. And just because a certain number of GPUs make you 1000mh on one coin it doesn't mean that will be the same for others. for example, the amount of GPU's it takes to get 1000hm on Eth will be less than what it takes on Rvn coin.

Mining rigs

components

  • Motherboard
  • Processor (CPU)
  • Memory (RAM)
  • Storage Device (SSD or USB)
  • GPU
  • PCI-E Risers
  • Frame or Server Chasis
  • Network Connectivity

The number of PCI-E Risers depends on the number of GPUs. The number of GPUs you can put on your rig depends on the PCI-E slots your motherboard has.

TThe first step when making a mining rig is placing the motherboard on the frame. Next, you connect the PCI-E risers to the motherboard. once the risers are connected you connect the risers to the GPUs. Next, you connect the PSUs to the motherboard and to the GPUs to give them power. Finally when all the hardware is connected all one needs to do is download the software this consists of downloading windows, GPU drivers, and the mining algorithm. once all of that is done you just need to open the algorithm on the mining rig and edit the script to be able to connect your external crypto wallet to your rig.
